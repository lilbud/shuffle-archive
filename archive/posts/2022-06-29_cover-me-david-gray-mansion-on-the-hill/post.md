How big of a Springsteen fan is David Gray?

Big enough that on his 2007 live album, *A Thousand Miles Behind*, he included not one but *two* covers of Bruce's songs.

We listened to one of them–an[affecting cover](../2020-02-08_cover-me-david-gray-streets-of-philadelphia/post.md) of "[Streets of Philadelphia](../2021-06-30_roll-of-the-dice-streets-of-philadelphia/post.md)"–a couple of years ago.

It's the other one that speaks to Gray's deep appreciation, though, because it's a song you'll almost never hear on the radio: "[Mansion on the Hill](../2022-03-06_roll-of-the-dice-mansion-on-the-hill/post.md)" from *Nebraska.*

[Watch on Youtube: David Gray - Mansion On The Hill (Official Audio)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlSAq-op000)

On the live album, it's overshadowed by the more well-known (and frankly stronger performance of) "Streets of Philadelphia," but fortunately that wasn't the only time Gray covered "Mansion on the Hill" live and captured it for posterity.

In 2019, Gray stopped by Sirius XM to appear on the Howard Stern show, and "Mansion on the Hill" was one of the songs he performed live in the studio. Twelve years on from his live album, Gray turns in a much stronger performance with an arrangement that superbly captures Bruce's *Nebraska* vibe, especially in the wordless bridge.

[Watch on Youtube: David Gray Covers Bruce Springsteen's "Mansion on the Hill" in Howard Stern's Studio](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=68SkOBc20HE)

Gray is no stranger to Bruce's *Nebraska* material; he's been known to play "[Atlantic City](../2018-06-04_roll-of-the-dice-atlantic-city/post.md)" and "[Used Cars](../2019-06-13_roll-of-the-dice-used-cars/post.md)" live as well.

[Watch on Youtube: David Gray - Atlantic City(Live In Zurich 2010)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YvY3Z7bmfeI)

Gray is [on tour this summer](https://www.davidgray.com/tour)–will he break out a new Springsteen cover when he plays New York and Philly (no New Jersey stop this year)? Odds are pretty decent, I think.
