***New for 2025:** New entry for 1979 and 2024; audio for 1988*

**1971:** Three–count ’em–*three* Asbury Park shows on this date–first at 1am with the Red Hot Mammas, then an afternoon gig with The Sundance Blues Band at the Student Prince, followed by an evening show with the Red Hot Mammas at The Upstage. (Bruce actually plays a *fourth* show at 1am, but that’s technically the following day.)

**1979:** Bruce and the E Street Band work on “[Jackson Cage](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/11/04/roll-of-the-dice-jackson-cage/)” at the Power Station in New York City.

**1984:** Bruce and the E Street Band rehearse for the upcoming Born in the U.S.A. Tour at the former home of Big Man’s West in Red Bank.

**1988**: Night Four of five at the final U.S. stand for the Tunnel of Love Express Tour at Madison Square Garden. This show features the tour premiere of “[Lonely Teardrops](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/01/22/cover-me-lonely-teardrops/)” as the final encore.

**1997:** Bruce performs a solo acoustic show at Teatro Augusteo in Naples, Italy, but the true highlight from this date happens afterward: the crowd sticks around outside the theater and sings “O Sole Mio”(which Bruce performed briefly during the show), prompting Bruce to appear on the theater’s balcony with Kevin Buell to play and lead the crowd in a sing-along of “Thunder Road.” Watch this moment–it’s magical.
