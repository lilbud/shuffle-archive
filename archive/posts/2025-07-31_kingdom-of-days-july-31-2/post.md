**1984:** “[Cover Me](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/01/13/roll-of-the-dice-cover-me/)” is released as the second single off of the red-hot *Born in the U.S.A.* album. It will peak at #7 on the charts.

**1992:** The opening stand of World Tour 1992 reaches its midpoint in East Rutherford on Night Six of eleven. Many tour premieres tonight, including “[Atlantic City](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/06/04/roll-of-the-dice-atlantic-city/),” “[Spirit in the Night](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/03/01/roll-of-the-dice-spirit-in-the-night/),” “[I’m on Fire](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/09/11/roll-of-the-dice-im-on-fire/)“, and the live debuts of “[Pony Boy](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/03/01/roll-of-the-dice-cover-me-pony-boy/)” and “[Cross My Heart](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/12/29/roll-of-the-dice-cross-my-heart/).”

**2001:** Bruce makes a surprise guest appearance at the Count Basie Theater in Red Bank, joining Bruce Hornsby for the final encore of “[Cadillac Ranch](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/07/23/roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch/).”

**2008:** At the final of three Giants Stadium shows on the Magic Tour, Bruce tour premieres “[Pretty Flamingo](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/12/04/cover-me-pretty-flamingo/)” and “[Jersey Girl](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/07/24/cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl/)” and holds a mini-Seeger Sessions reunion when Jeremy Chatzky, Sam Bardfeld, and Curtis King join the band for “[American Land.](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/01/10/roll-of-the-dice-american-land/)”

**2012:** Bruce wraps up the first European leg of the Wrecking Ball Tour with a marathon of a show in Helsinki–his longest ever, over four hours–and that’s not even including a five-song pre-show! Five songs have their tour premieres, including “[Back in Your Arms](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/11/10/roll-of-the-dice-back-in-your-arms/)“, “[Detroit Medley,](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/12/27/cover-me-detroit-medley/)” and covers of “[I Don’t Want to Go Home](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/03/30/meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-and-steve-van-zandt-i-dont-want-to-go-home/),” “Rockin’ All Over the World,” and “[(Your Love Keeps Lifting Me) Higher and Higher](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/09/23/cover-me-your-love-keeps-lifting-me-higher-and-higher/).” Plus: rare outings for “[Loose Ends](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/06/23/roll-of-the-dice-loose-end/),” “[Be True](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/12/02/roll-of-the-dice-be-true/),” “[Light of Day](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/12/06/roll-of-the-dice-light-of-day/),” and more–an absolutely amazing show. No surprise that it’s part of Bruce’s official archive series–get it [here](http://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,16397/Bruce-Springsteen---The-E-Street-Band-mp3-flac-download-7-31-2012-Olympiastadion-Helsinki-FI.html).
