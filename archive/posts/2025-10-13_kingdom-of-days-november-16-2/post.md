**1990:** Bruce plays the first of a pair of unique, remarkable benefit shows for the Christic Institute, a public interest law firm. This is Bruce’s first “official” concert since disbanding the E Street Band. He’s out of his comfort zone, and it shows. His life has changed: he’s firmly attached to Patti now (even if they’re not yet married), he’s a father, and his new life perspective informs the new work that comprises much of the setlist. World premieres abound: “[Red Headed Woman](../2021-09-15_roll-of-the-dice-red-headed-woman/post.md),” “[57 Channels (And Nothin’ On)](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/05/17/roll-of-the-dice-57-channels-and-nothin-on/),” “[When the Lights Go Out](../2021-01-23_roll-of-the-dice-when-the-lights-go-out/post.md),” and “[Real World](../2022-07-25_roll-of-the-dice-real-world/post.md)” all get their very first airings. Intimate performances of “[My Father’s House](../2021-08-13_roll-of-the-dice-my-fathers-house/post.md),” “[Brilliant Disguise](../1987-01-09_roll-of-the-dice-brilliant-disguise/post.md/)” and “[Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out](../2018-11-19_roll-of-the-dice-tenth-avenue-freeze-out/post.md)” (which sounds particularly forlorn tonight) take on new life. In the encores, Bonnie Raitt and Jackson Browne join Bruce for “[Highway 61 Revisite](../2018-11-07-meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-bob-dylan-and-friends-highway-61-revisited/post.md/)d” and a gorgeous rendition of “[Across the Borderline](../2022-03-26_matr-bruce-springsteen-bonnie-raitt-and-jackson-browne-across-the-borderline/post.md).” This show is available for purchase as an official archive series installment (get it [here](https://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,14250/Bruce-Springsteen-mp3-flac-download-11-16-1990-The-Christic-Shows-at-The-Shrine-Los-Angeles-CA.html)) and is essential for any Springsteen fan.<br>

**2002:** Bruce tour premieres “[Cadillac Ranch](../2020-07-23_roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch/post.md)” and plays a rare solo piano “[Incident on 57th Street](../2018-12-14_roll-of-the-dice-incident-on-57th-street/post.md)” at his Rising show in Greensboro, but it’s his fall during “[Waitin’ on a Sunny Day](../2019-09-27_roll-of-the-dice-waitin-on-a-sunny-day/post.md)” that almost ends up a literal show-stopper. Bruce stopped a serious fall by a deft save using Max’s drum stand, but the mishap was enough to draw blood, shocking fans who watched Bruce just continue on as if nothing had happened.<br>

**2010:** Bruce releases *The Promise: The Darkness on the Edge of Town Story* box set, featuring a new double-album of previously unreleased songs. That evening, he makes a guest appearance on *Late Night with Jimmy Fallon,* masquerading as “1975 Bruce” to perform “[Whip My Hair](../2019-09-05_matr-bruce-springsteen-and-jimmy-fallon-whip-my-hair/post.md)” with Fallon (who is masquerading as Neil Young). Once rid of the disguise, Bruce performs “Because the Night” (with Patti Smith’s lyrics) and “[Save My Love](../2019-07-16_roll-of-the-dice-save-my-love/post.md),” accompanied by Steve, Roy, and The Roots.<br>
