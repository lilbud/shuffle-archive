*New entry for 1977 and 2024; video for 2023***New for 2025:**

“Professor” Roy Bittan is born in Rockaway Beach, New York.

**1949:**



![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/roy-300x300.jpeg?resize=758%2C758)

The Castiles play an opening set for The Rogues at the new Surf ‘n’ See Club in Sea Bright, New Jersey.

**1966:**

The “Fabulous” Castiles play a gig at the See ‘n’ Surf Club (not a typo–same club, new name) to mark its first anniversary.

**1967:**



![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/1967-0702.jpg?resize=758%2C737)

The Sundance Blues Band plays the Upstage in Asbury Park.

**1971:**



![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/1971-07-02-300x102.jpg?resize=758%2C258)

Mike Appel sends Bruce a letter informing him that he is prohibited from recording new music due to their ongoing legal dispute. Bruce will spend 1976 and much of 1977 touring instead of recording as a result.

**1976:**

Bruce and the E Street Band record “” at Atlantic Studios in New York City, and Bruce works on a beautiful early solo piano arrangement of “.”

**1977:** [Don’t Look Back](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/05/03/roll-of-the-dice-dont-look-back/)[Racing in the Street](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/10/03/roll-of-the-dice-racing-in-the-street/)

[https://www.youtube.com/embed/pvMn2zFVX0E?feature=oembed](https://www.youtube.com/embed/pvMn2zFVX0E?feature=oembed)

Bruce and the E Street Band kick off the final leg of the River Tour by playing the first-ever event (by anyone) at the Meadowlands. Bruce debuts his cover of “” for the occasion; other highlights include the tour premiere of “” and the last-ever “Born to Run” opener.

**1981:** [Jersey Girl](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/07/24/cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl/)[I Don’t Want to Go Home](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/03/30/meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-and-steve-van-zandt-i-dont-want-to-go-home/)

The last of three nights in St. Paul (and the opening stand of the Born in the U.S.A. Tour) features the live debuts of “” and “,” along with the last full-band arrangement of “” for fifteen years.
