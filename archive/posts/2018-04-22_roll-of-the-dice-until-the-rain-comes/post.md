Today's entry is an unusual one. I've covered unreleased songs and even work-in-progress snippets, but this is the first essay for which we don't even have a scrap of music to listen to.

The only reason we know that "Until the Rain Comes" exists is that its lyrics were included in the 2006 public auction of a notebook containing lyrics from songs Bruce had written in 1968 at the tender age of eighteen.

![](../../assets/2019/01/1968_lyricsnotebook.jpg)

The table of contents shows several previously-unknown songs.

![](../../assets/2021/06/1968_lyricsnotebook-contentspage.jpg)

The ninth song, "Until the Rain Comes," is actually known to have been played by Bruce during his solo acoustic shows that spring and summer, but no recording has surfaced.

![](../../assets/2018/04/untiltheraincomes_lyrics.jpg)

The handwritten lyrics are above (thanks to springsteenlyrics.com), but here they are transcribed:

> *Today I sail from the shores of my homeland*<br>
> *reflections upon the water enchant my searching eyes*<br>
> *Across the flaming clouds rides the brave Apollo*<br>
> *until the rain comes leaving silence on the sky*<br>

> *I lay upon the soft green grass and think for many days*<br>
> *voices to my left and right tear me in two ways*<br>
> *Upon reaching the ancient age of 18 I have found*<br>
> *what is round isn't round at all, and what is up may be down*<br>

> *A windy day is running near, the white dove flies alone*<br>
> *broken brothers tear the earth and turn into dried up bones*<br>
> *I ask for an answer and do not hear a sound*<br>
> *lies upon lies again, until the rain comes*<br>

> *The emerald waves lap upon the shore*<br>
> *kiss the naked body of the night before*<br>
> *The sweet young sparrow weeps for things she's never done*<br>
> *and I'll drink her love, when the rain comes*<br>

> *So I cut the ropes that bind my craft to the shore*<br>
> *and sail upon the open sea, where freedom flows so pure*<br>
> *Back upon the sandy beach a child cries with dawn*<br>
> *I'll never return again, until the rain comes*<br>

Lyrically (and that's all we have to go on), "Until the Rain Comes" is very much the product of an 18-year-old, newly independent, questioning and unsure, saying goodbye to his childhood and venturing forth into adulthood.

Perhaps a recording will surface one day and we'll hear Bruce breathe life into the song.

**Until the Rain Comes**<br>
**Never Recorded**<br>
**First/Last Performed:** mid-1968<br>
