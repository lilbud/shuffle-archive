***New for 2025:** Audio for 1976; new entry for 2024*

**1972:** The still-unnamed E Street Band makes its concert debut as the Greetings from Asbury Park Tour kicks off with Bruce’s first-ever show in Pennsylvania, where they open for Cheech & Chong at West Chester College. Bruce was a late addition by the school, and Cheech & Chong’s manager does not approve. He forces the school to end Bruce’s set early.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/10/1972-1028-300x195.jpg)

**1976:** Bruce and the E Street Band kick off a six-night stand at the Palladium in New York City.

**1980:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of two River Tour shows at the Oakland Coliseum, where they open with “[Good Rockin’ Tonight](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/09/17/cover-me-good-rockin-tonight/).” They won’t play it again for 28 years.

**1984:** Bruce and the band play their third of seven Born in the U.S.A. shows at the Los Angeles Memorial Sports Arena, where “Rosalita (Come Out Tonight)” comes out for the last time that year.

**1992:** Bruce takes ill with a sore throat and postpones his scheduled show in St. Louis to December.
