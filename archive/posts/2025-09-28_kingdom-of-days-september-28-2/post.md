***New for 2025:** New entry for 1977*

**1968:** Bruce headlines at the Off Broad Street Coffee House in Red Bank, performing two acoustic solo set.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/1968-0928.jpg)

**1973:** The Wild & Innocent Tour kicks off at Virginia’s Hampden-Sydney College, where “[The E Street Shuffle](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/06/06/roll-of-the-dice-the-e-street-shuffle/)” makes its debut, featuring the band’s first-ever guest artist: Albee Tellone on baritone sax.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/1973-0928.jpg)

**1974:** Bruce’s scheduled show at Drew University in Madison, New Jersey (this blogger’s alma mater), is canceled less than 48 hours before showtime–because Bruce was booked at five other colleges for that same date. Rather than breach four contracts by choosing one, Bruce cancels on all five.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/1974-09-28a.jpg)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/1974-09-28b.jpg)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/1974-09-28c.jpg)

**1975:** Bruce plays in Kansas for the first time when the Born to Run Tour stops at Memorial Hall in Kansas City.

[Watch on Youtube: Bruce Springsteen - 1975-09-28 Memorial Hall, Kansas City, KS](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JcRLB5zG-dI)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/1975-0928.jpg)

**1977:** Bruce holds a recording session at The Record Plant in New York City, where he and the band work on “The Promise.”

[Watch on Youtube: Bruce Springsteen - The Promise (Unreleased Version #3)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ksRM63yzF2c)

**1985:** On *American Top 40,* host Casey Kasem shares an anecdote from one of Bruce’s bandmates from The Castiles.

**1986:** Bruce attends *Lady Beth*, a play by laid-off Freehold 3M plant workers at The Stone Pony, where he gives a short speech of support and encouragement.

[Watch on Youtube: Bruce Springsteen attends a play for 3M Workers - 1986-09-26](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QHUFaCmnesU)

**2004:** Bruce and the E Street Band rehearse at Asbury Park’s Convention Hall for their upcoming Vote for Change Tour.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/09/2004-09-28.jpg)

**2007:** Promoting their new *Magic* album and upcoming tour, Bruce and the E Street Band perform a live morning concert on NBC’s *Today*.

**2016:** In New York City, Bruce makes a promotional appearance at Barnes & Noble in Union Square in support of his newly published autobiography. Later, at the Soho Apple Store in New York City, Bruce is interviewed by Apple’s Eddy Cue*.* The event is simulcast on Facebook Live.

[Watch Video Highlight](https://videopress.com/embed/2IPVWGxy)

**2017:** To help offset high ticket prices, *Springsteen on Broadway* announces a daily lottery for a limited number of inexpensive tickets to the show.

[Watch on Youtube: 'Springsteen On Broadway' Lottery](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HWYbyDskN74)

**2018:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City. Patti is absent again tonight, so Bruce adds an extra encore of “This Hard Land” in place of his duet of “[Tougher Than the Rest](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/08/08/roll-of-the-dice-tougher-than-the-rest/)” with Patti.

**2019:** Bruce spends the day at his daughter’s equestrian competition in New York. That evening, he stops by his hometown of Freehold to visit the opening of the Monmouth County Historical Association Museum’s new exhibit, *Springsteen: His Home Town.* After touring the exhibit, Bruce surprises the assembled crowd by taking the stage with Joe Grushecky and The Houserockers for their opening number, “[Light of Day](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/12/06/roll-of-the-dice-light-of-day/).” For more highlights from this event, see my first-hand [*Where the Band Was* report](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/09/28/where-the-band-was-freehold-september-28-2019/).
