1972: Upon returning home from his aborted relocation to California, Bruce reunites with his eponymous band (now called The Sundance Blues Band) at The Captain’s Garter in Neptune, New Jersey.

1973: Bruce and the band play their final of four nights at My Father’s Place on Long Island.

1981: Night Two in Toronto on the River Tour. Listen to the show below.

1987: At the second Rock and Roll Hall of Fame ceremonies, Bruce inducts Roy Orbison and performs with him on “Oh, Pretty Woman.”

1996: The television news show 60 Minutes profiles Bruce and his solo acoustic tour.

2006: Bruce assembles the Seeger Sessions band for a final recording session, capturing “Eyes on the Prize,” “Jacob’s Ladder,” “Buffalo Gals,” “How Can I Keep From Singing” “Bring ’em Home” and “American Land.”  Of these, only “American Land” remains unreleased from this session. (Bruce re-recorded it for Wrecking Ball.)

2022: Bruce drops by the Transparent Clinch Gallery in Asbury Park, where he poses for a photo with the gallery’s owner (and himself). (Photo credit: Maria Barba-Clinch/Transparent Gallery Instagram)
