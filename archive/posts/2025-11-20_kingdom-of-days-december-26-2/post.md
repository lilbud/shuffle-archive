**New for 2025:** No updates<br>

**1975:** <br>
Bruce drops by the Bottom Line in New York City to catch Patti Smith's late show, providing backing vocals for "[Gloria](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/03/16/cover-me-gloria/)."

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/main/assets/2018/12/1975-1226-300x191.jpg.webp)

**1995:**<br>

Bruce announces an extension of his solo acoustic tour in the new year, with select dates in North America before heading overseas.

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lilbud/shuffle-archive/refs/heads/main/assets/2021/12/1995-12-2t6.jpg.webp)

**2017:**<br>
Patti Scialfa is sick with the flu and cannot perform, throwing a monkey wrench into the *Springsteen on Broadway* storyline and set list. Bruce adapts by changing that part of the show to focus on his kids instead of his wife, and accordingly, "[Long Time Comin'](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/06/19/roll-of-the-dice-long-time-comin/)" makes its first appearance in the show, in the spot where "[Tougher Than the Rest](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/08/08/roll-of-the-dice-tougher-than-the-rest/)" would normally be. Bruce also swaps out "[Brilliant Disguise](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/02/06/roll-of-the-dice-brilliant-disguise/)" for "[The Ghost of Tom Joad](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/10/17/roll-of-the-dice-the-ghost-of-tom-joad/)," giving added weight to the segment on his social values.
