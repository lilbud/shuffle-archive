New for 2025: No updates

1971: The Bruce Springsteen Band is in residence at the Student Prince in Asbury Park.

1975: Bruce and the E Street Band make a Born to Run Tour stop at Seton Hall University, where they play “Party Lights” for the last time to date.

1980: The original River Tour stops at the Civic Center in Providence, Rhode Island. The show is filmed–watch highlights from it below.

1984: Bruce and the E Street Band bring the Born in the U.S.A. Tour to Lexington, where they add “My Old Kentucky Home” to “Detroit Medley” for the occasion.

1992: Inclement weather forces the postponement of a scheduled show in Pittsburgh to five days later.

1996: Bruce plays his 100th show of his solo acoustic tour at the Veterans Memorial Auditorium in Columbus.

1998: Bruce makes an appearance on Italian television, performing and sitting down for an interview in Bologna.

2002: Bruce and the E Street Band team up with the Max Weinberg 7 on Late Night with Conan O’Brien for a special holiday performance and one of Bruce’s all-time best television appearances.
