Child continues their opening stand at the Pandemonium in Wanamassa, New Jersey. The band will play here for much of April. Red Bank’s spotlights the band in today’s paper.

**1969:***Daily Register*



![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/1969-04-04.jpg?resize=758%2C803&amp;ssl=1)

Bruce and the band play a gig at Michigan State University. The school paper’s review is less than enthusiastic.

**1976:**

[https://www.youtube.com/embed/GSHBy3lfgWc?feature=oembed](https://www.youtube.com/embed/GSHBy3lfgWc?feature=oembed)

![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/1976-0404.jpg?resize=758%2C360&amp;ssl=1)

Bruce and the E Street Band record the base track for “Roulette” at the Power Station in New York City.

**1979:**

[https://www.youtube.com/embed/_QWRFI3M0mo?feature=oembed](https://www.youtube.com/embed/_QWRFI3M0mo?feature=oembed)

Mixing work continues on at New York City’s Power Station.

**1980:** *The River*

Bruce postpones a scheduled show in London to recover from the previous leg of the River Tour.

**1981:**



![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/1981-0318.jpg?resize=379%2C502&amp;ssl=1)

Bruce and the E Street Band play their final Australian show of the Born in the U.S.A. Tour in Melbourne. Bruce will not perform in the country again for twelve years.

Bad Seattle traffic delays Bruce’s show at the Tacoma Dome. Bruce makes up for it by delivering a scorching set, filmed in its entirety by a fan in the audience and paired with an IEM recording. **2006:**

An odd stunt to promote the Magic Tour in Sacramento: a local radio station runs a contest to pick the opening song. “Spirit in the Night” won.

**2018:**

[https://www.youtube.com/embed/iVXDxAvzphc?feature=oembed](https://www.youtube.com/embed/iVXDxAvzphc?feature=oembed)

Meanwhile, continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City. Among the attendees this evening: Tony Goldwyn and Oprah Winfrey.

*Springsteen on Broadway*



![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/oprah-winfrey.jpg?resize=583%2C566&amp;ssl=1)



![](https://i0.wp.com/estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/04/tony-goldwyn.jpg?resize=675%2C661&amp;ssl=1)

Bruce shakes things up in Inglewood with a setlist inspired by the return of Patti Scialfa to the E Street stage. Four tour debuts tonight, including the show-opening cover of “Boom Boom,” “Tougher Than the Rest,” “Fire” and “Jole Blon.”
