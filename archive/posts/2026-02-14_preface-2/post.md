I suppose I could have called this *Meeting Across the River Volume One*.

All of the essays in these pages originally ran under that banner (except for the two brand new ones, written expressly for this book).

But here’s a secret you probably figured out a long time ago: when it comes to organizational structure, my original *E Street Shuffle* blog was a complete mess.

The *Cover Me* series included a mix of songs where Bruce covered other artists, and songs where other artists covered him. In retrospect, I should have made those two different named cateogies.

Similarly, *Meeting Across the River* never really found its identity. In the earliest days, it featured team-ups between Bruce and another artist covering Bruce’s own material, until I realized that would inevitably collide with the *Roll of the Dice* series.

Then it became team-ups where Bruce and other artists covered someone else’s material. Sometimes the song was by the other artist, and sometimes the song was a cover for both of them.

And then there were the studio recordings where Bruce made a guest appearance on a song written, recorded, and released by someone else. Can we even call those covers?

So when I started the slow-roll transformation of blog to books, I decided to consolidate to just three series.

*Roll of the Dice* volumes would include all material originally released on a specific album, even if that meant including covers. I decided to also include notable covers by other artists of songs on that album.

*Kingdom of Days* would also include notable *Where the Band Was* entries where I thought the content was worth preserving.

And then there are the *Cover Me* books, which would include all of the songs by other artists that Bruce covered, plus all *Meeting Across the River* entries where the song in question isn’t one that Bruce authored.

So somewhat belatedly, that’s the explanation for the same-but-different banners that grace the original blog version of these articles and the ones here in print.

This volume features a hefty collection of team-ups, united by a loose theme: every essay focuses on a song originally recorded by (or at least closely associated with ) the featured artists.

It’s the largest Bookshelf Collection yet, and there’s enough material left over for an inevitable companion volume.

Many of the essays were lightly retouched (especially some of the earliest ones), and there are two new ones (“Sandpaper” and “Will the Circle Be Unbroken”) included as a thank you to the subset of my readers who have enjoyed exploring Bruce’s sea of live and studio collaborations along with me.

Thanks again for reading. Many more to come.
