***New for 2025:** New entry for 1979 and 2024*

**1966:** The Castiles co-headline with The Rogues at the Surf ‘n’ See Club in Sea Bright, New Jersey.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1966-0821.jpg)

**1973:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second night of a week-long residency at Oliver’s in Boston.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1973-0821.jpg)

**1975:** Bruce opens a three-night stand at Alex Cooley’s Electric Ballroom in Atlanta.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1975-0821.jpg)

**1976:** Bruce, the E Street Band, and the Miami Horns bring the Lawsuit Tour to the Palace Theater in Waterbury, Connecticut.

[Watch on Youtube: Waterbury 21/08/1976](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLJTyL9twk3zAjgbMwpEkMyYVEXwh-zM2O)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/1976-08-21.png)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1976-0821.jpg)

**1978:** Bruce and the boys kick off a three-night Darkness Tour stand at Madison Square Garden. Before the show, Bruce sits down for an interview with Joel Siegel (it will air the following night).

[Watch on Youtube: WABC Interview with Bruce Springsteen, 1978](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5AJeDj3og0A)

[Watch on Youtube: Madison Square Garden (8/21/1978)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLu8VBPtfGX1BMGlg5htzeREH7csgj0guB)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/1978-08-21.jpg)

**1979:** Bruce records “[Dollhouse](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/08/15/roll-of-the-dice-dollhouse/)” with the E Street Band at The Power Station in New York City. It will remain in the vault for almost two decades.

[Watch on Youtube: Dollhouse (Studio Outtake - 1979)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HpF_i71cqVQ)

**1981:** At the second of six nights at the Los Angeles Memorial Sports Arena on the River Tour, Flo and Eddie guest-star on “[Hungry Heart](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/12/21/roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart/).” (They also provided backing vocals on the original album track.)

[Watch on Youtube: Los Angeles 21/08/1981](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLJTyL9twk3zBsoSImHtGzvLwx-Z_eDw-P)

[Watch on Youtube: Bruce Springsteen - "Hungry Heart" w/Flo and Eddie - 1981-08-21](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xwVaag7j2KI)

**1985:** At the third of a four-night Giants Stadium stand (there will be two more later in the summer), “[Bobby Jean](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/06/19/roll-of-the-dice-bobby-jean/)” is recorded and eventually released on *Live 1975-85*.

[Watch on Youtube: Bobby Jean (Live at Giants Stadium, E. Rutherford, NJ - August 1985)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HsgPJoBLbFU)

**1987:** Bruce and Stevie share a stage again, when Bruce joins Steve and The Disciples of Soul at their Stone Pony gig for “[Native American](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/10/25/matr-little-steven-and-bruce-springsteen-native-american/)” and “Sun City.”

[Watch on Youtube: Bruce Springsteen & Little Steven- Native American Stone Pony](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=63bxYb-NsOw)

**1988:** Bruce joins Cats on a Smooth Surface on stage at The Stone Pony for “[Stand By Me](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/12/12/matr-u2-and-bruce-springsteen-stand-by-me/)” and “[Around and Around.](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/02/26/cover-me-around-and-around/)” (Photo credit: Debra Rothenberg)

**2002:** Bruce debuts the modern-day rock arrangement of “[Dancing in the Dark](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/12/25/roll-of-the-dice-dancing-in-the-dark/)” at his Rising Tour show in Tacoma. It will be an E Street Band tour staple ever after. Also tour premiering at this show: “[Ramrod](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/09/10/roll-of-the-dice-ramrod/).” For more highlights from this show, see my [*Where the Band Was*](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/08/21/where-the-band-was-tacoma-august-21-2002/)[report.](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/08/21/where-the-band-was-tacoma-august-21-2002/)

**2008:** Bruce takes a Magic Tour victory lap this week, with each show bringing its own share of surprises and rarities. Tonight’s show in Nashville features the first “[Good Rockin’ Tonight](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/09/17/cover-me-good-rockin-tonight/)” in 28 years, a rare “[Held Up Without a Gun](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/10/12/roll-of-the-dice-held-up-without-a-gun/)” and “[Loose Ends](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/06/23/roll-of-the-dice-loose-end/),” and a tour premiere cover of “[I Fought the Law.](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/04/01/matr-bruce-springsteen-and-don-henley-i-fought-the-law/)” Marah’s Dave Bielanko joins the band when Rosie comes out in the encores. This show is available as part of Bruce’s official archive series–buy or stream it [here](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLJTyL9twk3zCglog4rO23DhkYFmSYQjxS).
