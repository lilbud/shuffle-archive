***New for 2025:** New entry for 1977 and 1979*

**1969:** Child plays a gig at the Ocean Side Surf Club in Sea Bright, New Jersey.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/1969-0829.jpg)

**1970:** Bruce plays his first-ever show in Tennessee when Steel Mill opens for Roy Orbison, Ronnie Milsap and others at the 3rd Annual Nashville Music Festival. About 50,000 people attend, making this show the largest Bruce will play until he starts playing stadiums in the mid-1980s.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/1970-08-29-249x300.png)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1970-0829-228x300.jpg)

**1975:** Bruce cancels a scheduled show at the University of Miami.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/1975-08-29a-228x300.png)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/1975-08-29b-300x288.png)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/1975-08-29-300x152.jpg)

**1977:** Bruce and the band are in the studio, recording for what will become *Darkness on the Edge of Town*.

**1978:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of two nights at the Stanley Theater in Pittsburgh, where Bruce tour premieres “[It’s My Life](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/11/23/cover-me-its-my-life/)” and plays “[(I Heard That) Lonesome Whistle](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/12/07/cover-me-i-heard-that-lonesome-whistle/)” for the first and only time to date. The TV show *PM Magazine* airs a segment shortly afterward, featuring footage from this show–check it out below.

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eScrQVnfB34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eScrQVnfB34)

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NuJ-g62ekzc](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NuJ-g62ekzc)

**1979:** Bruce and the E Street Band record an alternate version of “[The River](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/03/04/roll-of-the-dice-the-river/)” at The Power Station in New York City.

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BjSsFvrrh7g](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BjSsFvrrh7g)

**1984:** Bruce and the boys wrap up their four-night stand at the Capital Center in Largo, Maryland.

[https://videopress.com/embed/lUINKgNF](https://videopress.com/embed/lUINKgNF)

**1992:** Night Two at The Philadelphia Spectrum on World Tour 1992 features the last “[All or Nothin’ at All](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/01/30/roll-of-the-dice-all-or-nothing-at-all/)” for seventeen years and a “Follow That Dream” show closer.

[https://videopress.com/embed/C5ZvQSju](https://videopress.com/embed/C5ZvQSju)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/1992-08-29.png)

**1995:** Bruce and Joe Grushecky sit down for an interview at Cheers in Long Branch to promote Joe’s upcoming Bruce-produced album, [*American Babylon*](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/10/17/american-babylon-a-retrospective/).

[https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL39D3CD52696EBA54](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL39D3CD52696EBA54)

**2002:** On a rainy night, Bruce and the E Street Band kick off the MTV Video Music Awards, performing “[The Rising](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/11/21/roll-of-the-dice-the-rising/)” outside Hayden Planetarium in New York City for the cameras followed by a private 75-minute concert in the rain.

[https://videopress.com/embed/DJSBoofS](https://videopress.com/embed/DJSBoofS)

[https://videopress.com/embed/GLcWAlPv](https://videopress.com/embed/GLcWAlPv)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/2002-08-29-300x272.png)

**2012:** Bruce and the band play an unusual venue: the Vernon Downs Raceway, a horse racing track in central New York State. Bruce delivers a great set list, tour premiering [“Stand On It](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/10/08/roll-of-the-dice-stand-on-it/)” and “[Human Touch](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/08/09/roll-of-the-dice-human-touch/)” and giving rare outings to “[Frankie](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/03/31/roll-of-the-dice-frankie/),” “[I’m a Rocker](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/09/18/roll-of-the-dice-im-a-rocker/),” the 1978 version of “[Prove It All Night](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/10/24/roll-of-the-dice-prove-it-all-night/)” and “[Open All Night](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/10/28/roll-of-the-dice-open-all-night/).”
