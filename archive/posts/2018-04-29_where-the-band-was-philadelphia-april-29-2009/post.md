It's a rule that Night Two of a two-night stand is always better than [Night One](../2018-04-28_where-the-band-was-philadelphia-april-28-2009/post.md). Bruce tends to play it safe on the opening night and let loose on the last night.

But in Philly, *all* nights are special, and at these first two of six shows on the Working on a Dream Tour (the other four were six months away), it's a toss-up as to which was the stronger of the two.

I'll set "[The Fever](../2020-10-04_roll-of-the-dice-the-fever/post.md)" aside, because nothing would top the exhilaration I felt hearing that for the first time. The only thing that would come close is hearing "[Kitty's Back,](../2022-01-22_roll-of-the-dice-kittys-back/post.md)" which moved to the number one spot on my wishlist when I retired "The Fever" 24 hours prior.

What did Bruce deal Philly in the wild card pack on this night? First up, the E Street Band premiere of The Clash's "[London Calling,](../2018-04-09_matr-bruce-springsteen-elvis-costello-steve-van-zandt-dave-grohl-london-calling/post.md)" played by Bruce only once prior, at the Grammy Awards six years earlier; next, a rollicking arrangement of "[Red Headed Woman](../2021-09-15_roll-of-the-dice-red-headed-woman/post.md)" to welcome Patti back to the stage (she'd missed a few shows due to an injury), and finally the best performance of "[Thundercrack](../2021-09-24_roll-of-the-dice-thundercrack/post.md)" I've had the privilege to see (somehow, I've managed to catch this rarity five times).

You can watch all three wildcards below.

Next up: "[Waitin' on a Sunny Day](../2019-09-27_roll-of-the-dice-waitin-on-a-sunny-day/post.md)" gets a night off, with "[Hungry Heart](../2022-12-21_roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart/post.md)" a welcome substitute. Bruce's mom even contributed some guest vocals:

[Watch on Youtube: Hungry Heart Springsteen Philadelphia 4.29.09](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7NfOruRmkgc)

"[The Wrestler](../2020-10-31_roll-of-the-dice-the-wrestler/post.md)" also took a break–Bruce swapped it for "[Streets of Philadelphia](../2021-06-30_roll-of-the-dice-streets-of-philadelphia/post.md)" instead–another song on my wish list.

[Watch on Youtube: Bruce Springsteen & The E Street Band - Streets Of Philadelphia - 29-04-09.mp4](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JLjI7cUGpvw)

Then came the encores, and once again, Bruce pitched Philly a bonus: "Thunder Road," dedicated to the late Harry Kalas, whose customer voiceover for Bruce sent the crowd roaring.

[Watch on Youtube: Bruce Sprimgsteen Live From the Philadelphia Spectrum 4-29-09 - Thunder Road with Harry Kalas Intro](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5aGxv2Y7Mto)

…but Bruce *still* wasn't done. After "[American Land](../2016-01-22_roll-of-the-dice-american-land/post.md)," I waited to see which final number Bruce would send us home with. It had been "Rosalita (Come Out Tonight)" the night before, so it was almost certain to be "[Glory Days](../1984-01-12_roll-of-the-dice-glory-days/post.md)" tonight. (He'd been alternating between them for most of the tour.) Or maybe it would be "[Dancing in the Dark](../1984-01-13_roll-of-the-dice-dancing-in-the-dark/post.md)"–that chestnut had made a few appearances in that slot, too.

But it wasn't any of them. On this night, Bruce pulled out a tour debut–and unbelievably, I got my #1 most-chased song only 24 hours after getting my previous one: Bruce sent us off into the night dancing to the strains of an epic 13-minute "Kitty's Back." It didn't seem like Bruce ever wanted to stop–there was even a reprise–and those last three minutes were among the most joyous I've ever experienced at a Springsteen show.
