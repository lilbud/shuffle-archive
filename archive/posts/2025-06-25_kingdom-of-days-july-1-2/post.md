**1977:** *Darkness on the Edge of Town*

Bruce and the E Street Band play their second of two nights at Berkeley Community Theater on the Darkness Tour, with Bruce’s parents and sister Pam in attendance. This show is available as part of Bruce’s official archive series–get it . Listen in on the soundcheck below to hear the band working out “New York City Serenade.” Had they played it, it would have been the only performance of the song between the Born to Run Tour and the Reunion Tour.

The Reunion Tour comes to a deeply satisfying close at the legendary tenth night at Madison Square Garden. Bruce plays “” for the first time in 22 years and closes the show with a re-written “” summarizing Bruce’s feelings about the occasion. According to the , Bruce also debuts a new song at this show, “Ugo Hearts,” but strangely it will not appear on any of the bootleg recordings. This show is available as part of Bruce’s official archive series (get it ), and much of it is featured in on both audio and video formats.
