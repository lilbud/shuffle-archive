***New for 2025:** No updates*

**1969:** Still transitioning from Child to Steel Mill, Bruce and the band open for Chicago Transit Authority at Randolph-Macon College.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/1969-11-21-196x300.jpg)

**1971:** The Bruce Springsteen Band is in residence at the Student Prince in Asbury Park.

**1974:** Rocker John Eddie catches his first Bruce Springsteen concert when Bruce and the band play a gig at Camden County College.

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/1974-11-21-196x300.jpg)

**1975:** Bruce plays his first-ever show in Sweden when the Born to Run Tour arrives in Stockholm. The sound quality isn’t great, but these are the first notes the E Street Band ever play in Sweden.

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zcOzsbqkHfs](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zcOzsbqkHfs)

**1978:** [*Greetings from Asbury Park, N.J.*](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2023/01/05/album-companion-greetings-from-asbury-park-n-j/)is certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of America.

[https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=OLAK5uy_me8xYcyUK7_zcjX-xFU5znBLMwachuxZ0](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=OLAK5uy_me8xYcyUK7_zcjX-xFU5znBLMwachuxZ0)

That evening, Bruce and the E Street Band bring the Darkness Tour to Northwestern University.

[https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLJTyL9twk3zAlsoNqOP-C0WgpTMJA_WDu](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLJTyL9twk3zAlsoNqOP-C0WgpTMJA_WDu)

**1986:** *Born to Run* is certified triple platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America.

[https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=OLAK5uy_nZs0gvllzGn7y9-5OuorN7W37HPrPGrn8](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=OLAK5uy_nZs0gvllzGn7y9-5OuorN7W37HPrPGrn8)

**1992:** Bruce takes ill and is forced to cancel his scheduled concert in Lexington only six hours before showtime. Meanwhile, Bruce’s former band plays without him (but with Darlene Love, Ronnie Spector, Mitch Ryder, and others) in Red Bank at a benefit to fight hunger.

**2005:** Bruce closes out his solo acoustic tour with a pair of shows at the Sovereign Bank Arena in Trenton. Tonight is Night One, and Bruce opens with a world premiere: a cover of Link Wray’s “[Rumble](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/02/15/cover-me-rumble/)” in memory of Wray. But the big surprise comes late in the set: the return of long-lost “[Song for Orphans](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/04/10/roll-of-the-dice-song-for-orphans/),” unplayed for 32 years, with support from keyboard tech Alan Fitzgerald.

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WkAei555KP8](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WkAei555KP8)

![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/2005-11-21-119x300.png)
