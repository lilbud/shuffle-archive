This is the inevitable companion volume I promised in *Cover Me Volume Three: With a Little Help From My Friends.* 

It collects the remainder of the *Meeting Across the River* posts featuring Bruce Springsteen teaming up with other artists on their own songs, including four brand-new entries never published on the blog: “Dustland,” “Did You Say Such a Thing,” “Barbara Ann,” and “Rose Tattoo.”

I’ve also included my marathon *American Babylon* interview with Joe Grushecky from October 2020 as sort of a “bonus track.” It was my second long-form interview with Joe, and it laced my long-time admiration of him as artist with genuine affection for him as a conversationalist.

We’d go on to have many more back-and-forths over the following years whenever I would sit down to write a *Meeting Across the River* essay on one of his songs, or a *Roll of the Dice* entry for one of his co-authored songs with Bruce.

One of the pleasures of writing the blog over the years was learning more about other artists and gaining greater appreciation for their work. As I flip through these pages, I see many songs I wasn’t familiar with before I started the Shuffle, and lots of new-to-me nuggets for ones that I thought I knew.

I hope you discover some new treats as you flip through this volume, too.
