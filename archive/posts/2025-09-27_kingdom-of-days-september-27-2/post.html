New for 2025: Audio for 1975, 1985, and 1988

1975: Bruce and the “East Side Band” play their first-ever show in Missouri when the Born to Run Tour stops at the Ambassador Theater in St. Louis. The set list features a 27-minute(!) “The E Street Shuffle.”

1977: At the Record Plant in New York City, Bruce records “Because the Night,” “Outside Looking In,” and “Down by the River.” It will take more than 30 years for Bruce to release the first two; the last one still hasn’t been released to this day.

Watch on Youtube: Outside Looking In

1980: Bruce and the boys rehearse for the upcoming River Tour at Clair Brothers Audio in Lititz, Pennsylvania, and the rehearsals are captured on video.

1985: The final stand of the marathon Born in the U.S.A. Tour begins at the Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum, where Bruce plays both “War” and “Janey, Don’t You Lose Heart” for the very first time. “War” will continue to be a staple through the Tunnel of Love Tour, but “Janey” won’t be played again for eight years. 2002: Milwaukee gets tour one-offs of “Mary Queen of Arkansas” and a show-closing “Little Queenie” (complete with “Are you loose?”) and a “No Surrender” tour premiere when the Rising Tour stops at the Bradley Center for a night.

Watch Video Highlight

2003: The Rising Tour is winding down, and each show of this last month is special. At Miller Park in Milwaukee (one year to the day since his last show in that city), Bruce opens with “Candy’s Room,” plays a rare “Brilliant Disguise” and “Across the Border,” and by request: a Danny Federici spotlight on “Beer Barrel Polka.”

2004: Bruce and the E Street Band rehearse at Asbury Park’s Convention Hall for their upcoming Vote for Change Tour.

2007: Sirius XM radio brings back E Street Radio for a return engagement to coincide with the release of Magic. At least as of this writing, it will never leave the air again.

2016: Bruce becomes a published author with the release of Born to Run, his autobiography. To promote the book, Bruce kicks off a national book tour at the Barnes & Noble in his hometown of Freehold, New Jersey.

2018: Springsteen on Broadway continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City. Patti is sick tonight, so Bruce Broadway-debuts “Two Hearts” in place of his usual “Tougher Than the Rest” duet with his wife. In attendance tonight: Robert Gordon and Simple Minds.

2021: Bruce interviews Little Steven for a streaming event the following day, promoting Van Zandt’s new book, Unrequited Infatuations.
