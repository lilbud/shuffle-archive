Two years gone to the day, but his music lives on. Enjoy one of the earliest covers of a Bruce Springsteen song (and a darn good one): David Bowie's cover of "[It's Hard to Be a Saint in the City.](../2011-01-09_roll-of-the-dice-its-hard-to-be-a-saint-in-the-city/post.md/)"

[Watch on Youtube: David Bowie-It's Hard To Be A Saint In The City](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_pDYK4Ns-co)

Bowie recorded this in 1974, but it languished in the vaults until he released it on his *Sound + Vision* box set in 1989.

Although Bowie recorded this very early in Bruce's career, it was actually the second time he'd recorded one of Bruce's songs. ([The first](../2011-02-01_cover-me-david-bowie-growin-up/post.md/) was "[Growin' Up.](../2011-01-03_roll-of-the-dice-growin-up/post.md/)")

And the two icons hadn't even met yet! For the story of that first meeting, see [that day's Kingdom of Days entry](../2025-11-25_kingdom-of-days-november-25-2/post.md/).
