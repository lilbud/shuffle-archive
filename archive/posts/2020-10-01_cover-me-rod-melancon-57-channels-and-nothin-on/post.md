This.

For anyone who thinks "[57 Channels (And Nothin' On)](../2020-05-17_roll-of-the-dice-57-channels-and-nothin-on/post.md)" sizzles on vinyl but fizzled on stage, *this* is how you capture the dystopian alienation of Bruce's original without distorting it into the production number it became during Bruce's 1992-1993 tour. Courtesy of Red Melancon and his band:

[Watch on Youtube: Rod Melancon - 57 Channels (And Nothin' On) (Bruce Springsteen cover live Hotell Hulingen)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qQzD62E0CTk)

Melancon did more than just cover "57 Channels" live–he released it on his latest album, *Pinkville*, as well.

[Watch on Youtube: 57 Channels](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dB62gNbmJAg)

*Pinkville* is a strong collection of gritty southern rock, featuring a collection of original songs dark and twisted enough to sound like an electric *Nebraska*.

Rod's cover of "57 Channels (An Nothin' On)" fits so seamlessly on the album that it makes you realize just how at home the original would have been on *Nebraska* as well, in a different arrangement.

For more music from Melancon, check out his official site [here](https://www.rodmelancon.com/).
