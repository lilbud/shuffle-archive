**1973:** Bruce and the E Street Band headline at The Main Point in Bryn Mawr, Pennsylvania. Bruce is no stranger to having his name misspelled by now, but this is a new one.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/mainpoint-1-300x176.jpg?resize=758%2C446)
**1978:** Bruce sits down for an interview with WNEW-FM DJ Dave Herman in San Diego. (The interview will air in September on the King Biscuit Flower Hour.) As with Mary Turner a few days earlier, Bruce is loose, talkative, and funny. This interview is definitely worth a listen.

**1981:** Bruce and the E Street Band close out their opening stand at the Brendan Byrne Arena in the Meadowlands, and Gary U.S. Bonds joins them on stage for “[Jole Blon](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/08/29/matr-gary-u-s-bonds-and-bruce-springsteen-jole-blon/)” and “[This Little Girl](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/09/06/rotd-matr-cover-me-gary-u-s-bonds-bruce-springsteen-the-e-street-band-this-little-girl/).” Bruce’s performance of “[Jersey Girl](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/07/24/cover-me-roll-of-the-dice-jersey-girl/)” will be released in 1984 as the B-side to “[Cover Me](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/01/13/roll-of-the-dice-cover-me/)” as well as on the *Live 1975-85* box set. **1995:** One of the stranger collaborations of Bruce’s career: Bruce travels to Berlin, where he plays a short concert at Cafe Eckstein with Wolfgang Niedecken and His Leopardefellband. The purpose of the show is to yield a new recording and video for “[Hungry Heart](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/12/21/roll-of-the-dice-hungry-heart/),” which will be released on video and on the *Hungry Heart* EP (sort of–it will actually feature Bruce’s new vocals over the original instrumental track, and it’s kind of jarring. Compare the official video with the raw footage below). The setlist also features several covers, including Dylan’s “[Knockin’ on Heaven’s Door](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/02/18/matr-bruce-springsteen-and-wolfgang-niedecken-knockin-on-heavens-door/)” and “[Highway 61 Revisited](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/11/07/meeting-across-the-river-bruce-springsteen-bob-dylan-and-friends-highway-61-revisited/).”

**2013:** In town a day early for his show in Rome, Bruce catches Sting’s gig at Stadio del Tennis. After the show, Sting’s pianist David Sancious join his current and old bosses for a late dinner at Bruce’s hotel.
