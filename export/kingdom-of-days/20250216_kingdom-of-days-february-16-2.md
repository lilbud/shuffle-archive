**1973:** Bruce and the band play Monmouth College in West Long Branch, New Jersey. Tickets are only one dollar if you bring along a newspaper to recycle.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/1973-02-16.png)
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/1973-02-16-300x288.jpg?resize=758%2C729)
**1975:** *The Philadelphia Inquirer* does its best to wake the world beyond Philly and Texas to Springsteen’s power and talent. It won’t take much longer.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/The_Philadelphia_Inquirer_Sun__Feb_16__1975_-1-scaled.jpg)
**1977:** Bruce and the E Street Band play a gig at Veterans Memorial Auditorium in Columbus.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/1977-0216-300x233.jpg?resize=758%2C588)

**1983:** At The Hit Factory in New York City, Bruce records “Refrigerator Blues,” which to this date does not circulate.
**1985:** Bruce scores his first Number One album in the U.K. with *Born in the U.S.A*.
**1991:** Bruce joins Bruce Willis, Stephen Stills, and John McEnroe(!) on “[C.C. Rider](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/08/29/cover-me-c-c-rider/)” at the Tent on the Beach in Malibu.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/1991-02-16.png)

**2014:** Night Two in Melbourne on the High Hopes Tour features a full album performance of *Born to Run* and tour debuts of “[Lucky Town](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/05/24/roll-of-the-dice-lucky-town/),” “[Roulette](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/04/16/roll-of-the-dice-roulette/),” “[Growin’ Up](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/09/29/roll-of-the-dice-growin-up/),” [Lost in the Flood](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/02/20/roll-of-the-dice-lost-in-the-flood/),” and “[Meeting Across the River](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/08/04/roll-of-the-dice-meeting-across-the-river/).” Curiously, no official recording is available of this show.

**2017:** Night Two in Brisbane features the Australian premiere of “[Working on a Dream](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/07/04/roll-of-the-dice-working-on-a-dream/),” rare outings for “[Roll of the Dice](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/01/18/roll-of-the-dice-roll-of-the-dice/),” “[Jole Blon](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/08/29/matr-gary-u-s-bonds-and-bruce-springsteen-jole-blon/),” and “Follow that Dream,” and a guest audience member for “[Growin’ Up.](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/09/29/roll-of-the-dice-growin-up/)” Also tour premiering tonight: “[Long Time Comin’](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/06/19/roll-of-the-dice-long-time-comin/)” and “[Fire](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/08/05/roll-of-the-dice-fire/).”

**2023:** COVID-19 continues its tour across the E Street Band, moving on from Nils (who returns to the stage) and taking temporary residence in Jake. E Street Horns saxophonist Ed Manion steps up for double-duty, and the band soldiers on in Austin. “[Cadillac Ranch](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/07/23/roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch/)” makes its tour premiere tonight. “[Don’t Play That Song](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2023/02/12/cover-me-dont-play-that-song-you-lied/),” an early highlight, is dropped from the set and won’t appear again.
