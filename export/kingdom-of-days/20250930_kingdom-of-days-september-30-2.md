***New for 2025:** Audio for 1976; new entry for 1977*
**1967:** The Castiles play their second and final gig at The Left Foot in Freehold, where the setlist included covers like “[The Letter,](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/07/16/cover-me-the-letter/)” and [“San Franciscan Nights](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/05/22/cover-me-san-franciscan-nights/),” as well as a couple of Castiles originals, like “[Mr. Jones](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/02/01/roll-of-the-dice-mr-jones/).”

**1973:** Bruce’s acoustic guitar is stolen at a Wild & Innocent gig at Stony Brook University. Bruce will play road manager Albee Tellone’s guitar (the same model as Bruce’s) until Mike Appel replaces it as a Christmas gift.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/1973-0930-300x154.jpg?resize=758%2C389)
**1975:** Bruce makes his debut appearance in Nebraska, playing a Born to Run show at Omaha’s Civic Auditorium Music Hall.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/1975-0930.jpg)
**1976:** At Night Two on the Lawsuit Tour in Santa Monica, Bruce tells a mid-song “[Growin’ Up](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/09/29/roll-of-the-dice-growin-up/)” story for the first time. Later that night, Bruce and Steve make a surprise guest appearance on stage at Dion DiMucci’s show at The Roxy, providing backing vocals on “[A Teenager in Love.](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/08/16/matr-dion-bruce-springsteen-paul-simon-lou-reed-james-taylor-billy-joel-and-reuben-blades-a-teenager-in-love/)”

**1985:** Bruce and the E Street Band play their third of four shows on the Born in the U.S.A. Tour’s final stand in Los Angeles. “[Born in the U.S.A.,](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/09/21/roll-of-the-dice-born-in-the-u-s-a/)” “[Darlington County](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/09/10/roll-of-the-dice-darlington-county/),” “[Seeds](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/10/10/roll-of-the-dice-seeds/),” “[The River](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/03/04/roll-of-the-dice-the-river/),” “[War](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/05/27/cover-me-war/),” “[The Promised Land](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/07/04/roll-of-the-dice-the-promised-land/),” “[My Hometown](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/04/09/roll-of-the-dice-my-hometown/),” and “[Cover Me](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/01/13/roll-of-the-dice-cover-me/)” from this show all make it to *Live 1975-85*, and “My Hometown” and “War” will surface on *Video Anthology* as well.

**2009:** Bruce kicks off a five-night stand at Giants Stadium with a brand-new song: “[Wrecking Ball,](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2022/08/12/roll-of-the-dice-wrecking-ball/)” written to honor the scheduled-for-demolition venue. Curt Ramm is on hand, and the set list is clearly constructed to be horn-friendly as a result: “[Tenth Avenue Freeze-Out](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/11/19/roll-of-the-dice-tenth-avenue-freeze-out/),” “[Meeting Across the River](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/08/04/roll-of-the-dice-meeting-across-the-river/),” and “[The E Street Shuffle](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/06/06/roll-of-the-dice-the-e-street-shuffle/)” all get outings tonight, along with a full-album performance of *Born to Run*. Willie Nile makes a guest appearance in the encores.
