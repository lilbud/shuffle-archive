# Kingdom of Days: April 20
#### Originally Published on Ken Rosen's E Street Shuffle blog at https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2025/04/20/kingdom-of-days-april-20-2/
#### Published: April 20, 2025
#### Last Updated: April 16, 2025
![baltimore16.jpg](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/baltimore16.jpg)

***New for 2025:** New entry for 1969, 1980, and 1983; video for 1999*
**1969:** Child performs at Hullabaloo in Asbury Park.
**1974:** Bruce and the still-unnamed E Street Band perform a truncated show (for fire safety reasons) at Ursinus College in Collegeville, Pennsylvania.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/04/1974-0420-300x269.jpg?resize=758%2C679)
**1976:** Bruce and the band play a sparsely attended show at the Freedom Hall Civic Center in Johnson City, Tennessee.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/1976-04-20-172x300.jpg?resize=758%2C1322)
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/04/1976-04-20b.jpg)
**1980:** Bruce works on mixes for his upcoming album *The River* at Clover Studios in Los Angeles.
**1981:** A scheduled River Tour show in Paris for this date was moved to April 18.
**1983:** Bruce works on “[Cynthia](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/09/06/roll-of-the-dice-cynthia/)” at his home studio in Los Angeles.

**1999:** Another live debut for Milan at Night Two in the Fila Forum: Bruce opens with the first-ever performance of “[I Wanna Be With You.](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/01/14/roll-of-the-dice-i-wanna-be-with-you/)” This is only the second Night Two of the tour, and Bruce breaks the set list open with the first performances of “[Atlantic City,](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/06/04/roll-of-the-dice-atlantic-city/)” “[Factory](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/10/31/roll-of-the-dice-factory/),” “[Sherry Darling](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/07/06/roll-of-the-dice-sherry-darling/),” “[Cadillac Ranch,](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/07/23/roll-of-the-dice-cadillac-ranch/)” and “This Hard Land” of the Reunion era.
