# Kingdom of Days: August 9
#### Originally Published on Ken Rosen's E Street Shuffle blog at https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2025/08/09/kingdom-of-days-august-9-2/
#### Published: August 09, 2025
#### Last Updated: July 22, 2025
![01-Summertime-Blues-mp3-image.jpg](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/01-Summertime-Blues-mp3-image.jpg)

***New for 2025:** New entry for 1973 and 1977; audio for 1978; video for 1985*
**1968:** The Castiles headline two shows at the Off Broad Street Coffee House in Red Bank.
**1972:** Bruce starts a six-night stand opening for Dave Van Ronk at Max’s Kansas City in New York.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1972-0809-300x191.jpg?resize=758%2C483)
**1973:** Bruce works on “[Thundercrack](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2021/09/24/roll-of-the-dice-thundercrack/)” and “[4th of July, Asbury Park (Sandy)](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/04/26/roll-of-the-dice-4th-of-july-asbury-park-sandy/)” at 914 Sound Studios in Blauvelt, New York.
**1974:** Bruce cancels an outdoor gig at the Tanglewood Music Festival in Lenox, Massachusetts to make room for ongoing *Born to Run* recording sessions.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/1974-08-09-287x300.png?resize=758%2C792)
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/1974-08-09-272x300.jpg?resize=741%2C817)
**1975:** Bruce and the E Street Band bring the Born to Run Tour to Pittsburgh’s Syria Mosque, where future friend and collaborator Joe Grushecky is in attendance.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/1975-08-09.png)
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/1975-0809-237x300.jpg?resize=758%2C959)
**1976:** Mike Appel’s lawsuit against Bruce gets underway, as a judge places a preliminary injunction on Bruce, prohibiting him from recording with Jon Landau.
**1977:** Bruce and the E Street Band hold a recording session for *Darkness on the Edge of Town* at Atlantic Studios in New York City.
**1978:** Bruce and the E Street Band play one of their best shows of the Darkness Tour at The Agora in Cleveland, a free anniversary show for local radio station WMMS, with tickets awarded by lottery. The show is broadcast live on WMMS and affiliate stations, and it is available today as part of Bruce’s official archive series–get it [here](http://live.brucespringsteen.net/live-music/0,11757/Bruce-Springsteen---The-E-Street-Band-mp3-flac-download-8-9-1978-The-Agora-Cleveland-OH.html).

Bowen guest-stars for a one-time-only (until 2011) cover of “[A Woman’s Got the Power](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/03/01/cover-me-a-womans-got-the-power/),” played for Bruce’s mom, who is in the audience along with his father.

**1991:** [*Greetings From Asbury Park, N.J.*](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2023/01/05/album-companion-greetings-from-asbury-park-n-j/)is certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America, eighteen years after its initial release.

**1996:** Bruce stops by the Great Northern Bar and Grill in Whitefish, Montana–a state in which he has never played before–and joins the house band on stage for two songs. The Fanatics have been hired for the wedding of Toby Scott, Bruce’s long-time sound man and archivist, so Bruce puts them through their paces.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/1996-08-09-300x264.png?resize=758%2C667)
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/1996-0809.jpg)
**1999:** Night Thirteen of fifteen at the Meadowlands sees the Reunion Tour premiere and one-off performance of “[Frankie](http://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2019/03/31/roll-of-the-dice-frankie/),” not heard from in 23 years. During “[Spirit in the Night](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2020/03/01/roll-of-the-dice-spirit-in-the-night/),” Bruce opens the show with “[Darkness on the Edge of Town](https://estreetshuffle.com/index.php/2018/04/10/roll-of-the-dice-darkness-on-the-edge-of-town/)” (the second and last time to date that song has ever opened a show) and invites lifelong friend (and recent conga convert) Steve Eitelberg to join him on stage, making good on an alcohol-fueled promise. **2017:** It’s official: Bruce Inc. confirms months of rumors and announces *Springsteen on Broadway* will debut in the fall. Fans worldwide immediately convulse with anxiety over ticket prices (as much as $850) and the new Verified Fan system, which promises to dampen secondary market resales.
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/2017-0809-300x169.jpg?resize=758%2C427)
![](https://estreetshuffle.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/2017-08-09-300x285.png?resize=758%2C720)
**2018:** *Springsteen on Broadway* continues its theatrical run at the Walter Kerr Theater in New York City.
**2023:** Bruce and E Street Band kick off the second domestic leg of their 2023 World Tour at Wrigley Field in Chicago.
